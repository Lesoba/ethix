How to Report Unethical Behaviour

Reporting is always a difficult decision to make and it takes moral courage to do so. However, it is in the best interest of your department and organisation to do so. It will also provide you, as the reporting individual, with a positive sense that you are doing what is right.

Unethical behaviour that goes unreported ultimately affects morale, the organisational culture, profitability and often causes financial loss to the organisation if not addressed.

It is important for your own peace of mind, and to protect the organisation’s reputation and thus your job, that you report unethical behaviour that you may have personally observed or heard about.

It is your duty to speak up!

  • Internal Reporting

Engage with the right person inside your company. You can report unethical behaviour by engaging with your HR department, Internal Audit, your organisation’s dedicated Ethics Officer or the executive assigned to deal with ethics matters.

  • External Reporting

Alternatively, you could use the official whistleblowing line. This is a channel that allows employees and others to confidentially and anonymously alert the organisation about suspicions of misconduct.
